    A 13-Week Cash Flow View

    Cash problems don’t show up suddenly — they’re created weeks in advance by decisions made without visibility.

    Weeks 1–4

    Committed Cash

    These are obligations already in motion.

    • Payroll
    • Rent
    • Vendor payments
    • Debt service
    Action: confirm timing and avoid surprises.
    Weeks 5–9

    Decision Window

    Choices here shape cash before it hits.

    • Hiring timing
    • Vendor delays
    • Pricing changes
    • Expense throttling
    • Collection pressure
    Action: decide early and protect runway.
    Weeks 10–13

    Risk Zone

    Where uncertainty compounds fast.

    • Cash dips
    • Credit reliance
    • Deferred payments
    • Stress reactions
    Action: reduce volatility and build options.

    Most cash “surprises” are visible weeks earlier — if you’re looking in the right window.

    Forecast → Action

    Forecasting only matters when it changes decisions. This is the operating loop that turns numbers into action.

    The failure point: most businesses stop at Forecast. Forecast to Action closes the loop with a repeatable cadence.

    If your forecast isn’t changing decisions, it’s not a forecasting problem — it’s an operating rhythm problem.

    Pro Forma

    From “We Think It Works” to Decision-Grade Clarity

    Most pro formas are built to confirm a plan. This process is built to protect the decision.

    Common Starting Point
    Model-as-justification
    • Assumptions are implied, not explicit
    • Upside is clear, downside is vague
    • Cash timing is missing or simplified
    • Execution risk is under-modeled
    • “It feels right” substitutes for stress tests
    The Decision Process
    A repeatable framework that turns a pro forma into a decision tool.
    1 Decision clarity
    2 Key assumptions
    3 Downside visibility
    4 Timing & cash impact
    5 Risk concentration
    6 Operational reality
    7 Decision thresholds
    Decision-Grade Clarity
    Model-as-decision tool
    • Assumptions documented and testable
    • Downside is quantified (not hand-waved)
    • Cash runway and timing are visible
    • Execution requirements are explicit
    • Clear “go / pause / stop” thresholds
    The payoff
    You can defend the decision to partners, lenders, or your own future self — because the model has been forced to tell the truth.
